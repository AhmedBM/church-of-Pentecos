The Church of Pentecost

A Journey of Faith, Growth, and Spirit

Our Beginnings

The Church of Pentecost traces its roots to 1937 with the arrival of Pastor James McKeown, an Irish missionary sent by the Apostolic Church, Bradford, UK. Facing challenges in the Gold Coast, doctrinal disagreements led to the formation of a new movement grounded in faith, resilience, and divine healing.

1937

Pastor James McKeown arrives in the Gold Coast.

1939

Group splits over doctrinal differences into Christ Apostolic Church and Apostolic Church, Gold Coast.

1953

Founding of the Gold Coast Apostolic Church.

1962

The name "The Church of Pentecost" is officially adopted.

Beliefs and Tenets

  • The divine inspiration and authority of the Bible.
  • Existence of the One True God in the Triune Godhead.
  • Man’s need for salvation through Jesus Christ.
  • The sacraments of Baptism and the Lord’s Supper.
  • Baptism and Gifts of the Holy Spirit.
  • Divine Healing provided through atonement.
  • Faithfulness in tithes and offerings.
  • The Second Coming of Christ and Eternal Life.
